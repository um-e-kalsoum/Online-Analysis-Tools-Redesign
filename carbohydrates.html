Carbohydrates

In addition to the more common genomic and proteomics we are now seeing other molecular disciplines including transcriptomics (mRNA), metabolomics (metabolites) lipidomics (lipids) and glycomics (carbohydrates). These pages provide useful information on the structure to mono to oligosaccharides.

It is important to recognize the great diversity of monosaccharides commonly encountered in animals, plants, and microbes, as well as to organize them in a visually interesting style that also emphasizes their similarities and relatedness. This article discusses the nature of building blocks, monosaccharides, and monosaccharide derivatives—terms commonly used in discussing “glycomolecules.”

See Cummings’ Periodic Table of Monosaccharides (Reference: Cummings RD (2024) Glycobiology, 34(1): cwAd088). GlyTouCan is the international glycan structure repository, a freely available registry assigning unique accession numbers to glycan structures globally. GNOME provides semantic browsing and classification for glycan structures.

  • GlyGen — integrates global carbohydrate and glycoconjugate data from multiple international databases. (Reference: York WS et al. (2019) Glycobiology 30(2): 72–73).
  • GLYCAM (Robert J. Woods, Complex Carbohydrate Research Center, University of Georgia) — provides visualization tools for glycan-antibody and protein-glycosylation analysis.
  • CSDB (Carbohydrate Structure Database) — merged from BCSDB and PFCSDB, includes manually curated carbohydrate structures and literature. (Reference: Ph.V. Toukach & K.S. Egorova, Nucleic Acids Res 44(D1): D1229–D1236).
  • GLAD (Glycan Array Dashboard) — a web-based visualization tool for glycan microarray data. (Reference: Mehta YA & Cummings RD (2019) Bioinformatics, 35(18): 3536–3537).
  • GlycoGlyph — a web app for drawing and exporting glycan structures as SVG or GlycoCT. (Reference: Mehta YA & Cummings RD (2020) Bioinformatics, 36(11): 3613–3614).
  • ChemSpider — a chemical database with 100M+ structures from the Royal Society of Chemistry.
  • Chem-Space — uses Marvin JS for chemical drawing and database search (1.8B chemicals).
